# Res Continens

The cryptoclass of **containers and bounded interiors**. Categorising
feature *вместилищность* / *capacity to enclose* — Zadobrivskaya's
'hollow object of stable form intended for some content'. **Not
Spanish-specific**: this is the **eighth English cryptoclass**,
established for English by Zadobrivskaya (2019) over the same 23 emonyms
× 20 variants as Donina (see `notes/theory-zadobrivskaya.md`). It is
one of the two classes the project carries *beyond* Boriskina's
original six. What is **provisional is its weight in the *Spanish*
emonym profile**, not the existence of the class: its admission to the
project's working inventory is **conditional on the Varimax factor
analysis in ROADMAP §Phase 6** — see *Provisional status and the
factor-analysis gate* below.

For the framework that grounds this file see
`notes/theory-boriskina.md`. The Spanish classifier-seed inventory lives
in `data/classifiers.tsv` (rows where `cryptoclass = "Res Continens"`).
This file is the **commentary** layer plus the **defence of
inclusion** — given the class is provisional, the documentation has to
carry the burden of justifying it.

## Prototype

- **Spanish (project canonical)**: *recipiente* — see
  `data/cryptoclasses.tsv`. Also *caja*, *vasija*, *cuenco*, *frasco*.
- **No Boriskina prototype**, but **not a project invention**:
  Zadobrivskaya (2019) gives the English prototype names *cup, glass,
  box, bowl, bucket, basket*. The conceptual lineage is **Lakoff &
  Johnson's CONTAINER image schema** (*Metaphors We Live By* 1980, §6)
  and Rakhilina's *topotype* "container"; the cryptoclass framing is
  Zadobrivskaya's (for English). The project's contribution is the
  Spanish cross-variant application and the Spanish prototype
  *recipiente*.

For *recipiente* the [+container] feature is constitutive. Other
container nouns (*caja*, *bolsa*, *frasco*, *cuenco*, *botella*) are
also non-metaphorical members. Emonyms enter the class by analogy: *vivir
en el miedo*, *caer en la tristeza*, *está lleno de amor*, *sacar a
alguien de la depresión*, *guardar el amor en el corazón*.

## Provisional status and the factor-analysis gate

Two prior commitments shape the class's admission:

1. **Contrastive principle** (Boriskina §3, p. 49–52): a candidate
   cryptoclass is admissible if some natural language somewhere marks
   the categorising feature overtly. For containment this is **firmly
   satisfied** — Zadobrivskaya (2019) catalogues overt container
   markers in Russian (*-ница*: *чернильница, сахарница, пепельница*),
   Avar (a local case for 'being inside a cavity that can contain'),
   Navajo (classifier *khǫ* for 'container with hard edges'), Atsugewi
   (*into*-type suffixes), and Upper Kuskokwim (grammatical classes for
   stable- vs. unstable-form containers); plus the Australian/Papuan
   systems already noted (Aikhenvald 1998). See
   `notes/theory-zadobrivskaya.md` §3. **Spanish strengthens this from
   within its own morphology** — the container suffix *-ero/-era*
   (*azucarero, salero, tintero, cenicero*) is the direct cognate of
   Russian *-ница*. The typological warrant is no longer "suggestive
   but inconclusive"; it is solid.
2. **Empirical resolution by Zadobrivskaya**: the "strong
   Res-Continens-like patterns" Donina noted in English but had no
   class for (since she stayed at Boriskina's 6;
   `notes/methodology-donina.md` §8) were exactly what Zadobrivskaya
   (2019) went on to formalise as the 8th English class. All 23
   English emonyms tested positive as metaphoronyms of *Res Continens*,
   and it is the **dominant** image for 10 of them (incl. *fear* at
   71.51 %; `notes/theory-zadobrivskaya.md` §5). The Spanish project's
   inclusion of the class is therefore well-founded for English; the
   open Spanish question is purely how heavily *Spanish* emonyms load
   on it.

The class therefore enters the inventory **on probation**. The decision
gate is the **Varimax factor analysis** in ROADMAP §Phase 6
(`notes/methodology-donina.md` §7.4): Res Continens must load on its
own factor at ≥ 0.95 to survive. If it collapses onto Res Liquidae
(containers hold liquids — the obvious failure mode), it is dropped and
the statistics re-run on the inherited six classes (or seven, if
Res Planae survives but Continens doesn't). All Continens-related
analysis downstream of Phase 6 is therefore **contingent**.

## Cross-linguistic typological support

- **Zadobrivskaya (2019)** is the primary reference: overt container
  markers in Russian (*-ница*), Avar (local case), Navajo (*khǫ*),
  Atsugewi (*into*-suffixes), Upper Kuskokwim (stable/unstable
  container classes). See `notes/theory-zadobrivskaya.md` §3. **Spanish
  *-ero/-era*** (*azucarero, salero, tintero, cenicero*) adds a
  same-family overt cognate Zadobrivskaya could not cite.
- **Australian and Papuan classifier systems** mark containers as a
  distinct class (Aikhenvald 1998, *Classifiers*).
- **Lakoff & Johnson** (1980): CONTAINER is one of the foundational
  image schemas of conceptual metaphor — *embodied* in the body-as-
  container experience. The schema theory predicts that any
  sufficiently abstract noun-domain (emotions especially) will recruit
  container metaphors productively. The cryptoclass framing tests
  whether that recruitment is *systematic enough* to count as a
  covert grammatical category, not merely a productive metaphor.
- **The Spanish data itself** is the strongest argument: 499 citations
  across 9 construction types and 21 variants, second only to
  Res Liquidae. A class that productive is unlikely to be a coincidence
  — but factor analysis decides.

## Classifier inventory — Spanish

Canonical seeds in `data/classifiers.tsv` (nine rows for this class —
**the largest inventory of all eight classes**). The schema
(`data/SCHEMA.md`) introduced three locative slugs specifically for
Res Continens — without them the class's distinctive structure
collapses:

- `verbal-objective` — *tapar, destapar, llenar, vaciar* — acting
  on the container (capping, uncapping, filling, emptying)
- `verbal-subject-transitive` — *contener, tener, almacenar,
  guardar* — the container holds the emonym
- `verbal-subject-intransitive` — *(empty; rare for this class)*
- `verbal-instrumental` — *almacenar en, guardar en*
- `verbal-locative-state` — *estar en, encontrarse en, vivir en,
  existir en, aparecer en* — being inside the container
- `verbal-locative-into` — *caer en, entrar en, sumergirse en* —
  motion into the container
- `verbal-locative-out` — *sacar de, salir de* — motion out of the
  container
- `attributive` — *lleno de, rebosante de, vacío de*
- `predicative` — *está lleno de, está vacío de, está rebosante de*

The **three-way locative split** (state vs. into vs. out) is what
distinguishes Res Continens from any other class in the inventory.
Boriskina's original schema had a single locative category;
`data/SCHEMA.md` splits it because the three patterns behave
analytically differently for emonyms:

- *state* (`vivir en la tristeza`) frames the emotion as a durative
  inhabited environment
- *into* (`caer en la tristeza`) frames it as a destination with a
  threshold crossing
- *out* (`sacar a alguien de la tristeza`) frames it as a place
  someone is rescued from

Collapsing these would lose the verb-aspect / event-structure
distinctions that may be diagnostic for variant differentiation in
Phase 6.

## Construction-type breadth — keep in mind

Res Continens populates **8 of 9 construction types** (only the
subject-intransitive is empty, and that's noted as "rare in this
class" in the seed table). This is the **broadest classifier
inventory** of any of the eight cryptoclasses — a fact that itself
weighs in favour of the class surviving the Varimax gate: a feature
that recruits this many syntactically distinct construction types is
unlikely to be a noise factor.

Implication for Phase 2: **budget query effort heavily** for
Res Continens, and especially for the three locative slugs which
together account for the bulk of citations (see *Current data status*).
The locative-state slug alone (with seed lemmas *estar en*,
*encontrarse en*, *vivir en*, *existir en*, *aparecer en*) is the
single highest-yield query target in the entire class.

## Current data status

From `data/citations.tsv` (rows where `cryptoclass = "Res Continens"`):

- **499 citations** — second only to Res Liquidae.
- **Dominant patterns** (top tier):
  - `miedo verbal-locative-state` (empty lemma) — 85 rows. The
    largest single Continens cell.
  - `tristeza verbal-locative-into caer en` — 38.
  - `alegría verbal-locative-state` — 36.
  - `tristeza verbal-locative-into` (empty lemma) — 35.
  - `amor attributive lleno de` — 35.
  - `miedo verbal-objective tapar` — 28.
  - `miedo verbal-locative-state vivir en` — 24.
  - `miedo verbal-locative-into caer en` — 20.
- **Per-variant coverage**: ES 82, MX 80, AR 73, US 54, CO 46, VE 31,
  PE 29, CL 26. Central American coverage is **the worst** of any
  high-data class: GT 1, CR 2, HN 3, NI 8, PA 5, **SV zero**. The
  critical-mass threshold (≥ 5 per variant × cryptoclass cell) is met
  for ~ 9 variants out of 21 — meaning > half the variants will
  remain exploratory for Continens even after pipeline collection,
  unless the pipeline is heavily weighted toward Central American
  sources.
- **Per-emonym distribution**: *miedo* dominant (~ 35 % of class
  rows), driven by the *vivir en el miedo* / *caer en el miedo* /
  *tapar el miedo* triad. *amor* second (~ 22 %), driven by
  *lleno de amor* and *guardar amor*. *tristeza* third, *alegría*
  fourth, *ira* tail.

### Known extraction caveats

- **Empty-lemma rows in locative slots** (the 85 *miedo*
  locative-state rows and 35 *tristeza* locative-into rows are the
  worst offenders) are real citations the extractor pulled from
  column-header positions without identifying the specific lemma in
  the fragment. Spot-checking confirms most are *estar en* / *vivir
  en* / *caer en* — the lemma list captures them, but the
  per-citation lemma field is sparse and must be re-populated by
  the pipeline.

## Query-design hints for Phase 2

Pipeline-specific notes carried forward to `pipeline/README.md` when
that file is created:

- **`vivir en + N` is the highest-precision Continens query** for
  emonyms — almost every surface match is genuinely classificatory.
  Run first to build confidence against the gold set.
- **`caer en + N` is also high-precision** but has a literal
  spatial reading (*caer en la calle*) to filter against; emonym
  co-occurrence in the N slot is the discriminator.
- **`lleno de + N` overlaps with Res Liquidae's substantive
  classifier semantically** — *lleno de amor* is plausibly
  "container full of liquid emotion" rather than purely "container
  full of contents". This is the most important inter-class
  boundary in the project; see *Open questions* and
  [[res-liquidae]] §"Open questions". The query layer captures all
  *lleno de + emonym* matches; the LLM filter and ultimately the
  factor analysis decide assignment.
- **`tapar + N` and `destapar + N` are syntactically tight** —
  acting on the container. Few non-classifier readings exist for
  emonyms (you don't literally cover a fear). High-precision query.
- **`sacar de + N` has a frame-switching gotcha**: *sacar a
  alguien de la tristeza* (motion-out, classificatory) vs. *sacar
  un objeto de la caja* (literal). The animacy of the figure
  (person vs. thing) is the discriminator.
- **Guardar / almacenar are the lowest-frequency seeds** but the
  most semantically specific — they frame the emonym as something
  *kept* / *stored*. Worth a targeted high-recall query for
  *amor* (the dominant emonym for this pattern: *guardar amor en
  el corazón*).

## Open questions specific to Res Continens

- **Will Res Continens survive the Varimax gate?** The known failure
  mode is collapse onto Res Liquidae (containers hold liquids). If
  the loading on the Continens factor is < 0.95 and the cross-loading
  with Liquidae is > 0.3, the classes are not separable and Continens
  is dropped. Decision recorded in
  `notes/cryptoclasses/_inventory-decisions.md` post-Phase 6.
- **Is the `verbal-locative-out` slot a real Spanish gap or a
  collection oversight?** Only 3 citations of *miedo
  verbal-locative-out* and similar single-digits for other emonyms.
  Either *salir de la tristeza* / *sacar de la tristeza* is genuinely
  rare in spontaneous discourse, or the manual collection didn't
  query for it. The pipeline will resolve this.
- **Should *guardar / almacenar* (the "treasure-keeping" sub-
  pattern) be split from `verbal-subject-transitive` into its own
  sub-classifier?** Semantically it differs from *contener / tener*
  — *guardar amor en el corazón* implies cherishing, not just
  holding. If the pipeline returns enough rows, the split may be
  analytically warranted.
- **Is the `lleno de + emonym` attributive a Continens classifier or
  a Liquidae one?** *Lleno* primarily attributes to the container
  but the *de + N* slot has the emonym, not the container.
  Argument for Continens: *lleno* is a container property. Argument
  for Liquidae: the emonym is treated as fluid filling space. The
  factor analysis is the principled tie-breaker; the query layer
  must capture both.
- **Does Spanish *encontrarse en X* belong in the same construction
  as *estar en X*, or does it carry a distinct evidential framing
  (*encontrarse* implying a discovered state)?** Defer until
  per-citation lemma data is dense enough to compare distributions.
